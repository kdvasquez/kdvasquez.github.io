I’ve been wanting to travel for a while now. Usually, whenever I travel, it’s to visit family in Peru, my friend in Michigan, or somewhere local in California. Don’t get me wrong, this by no means diminishes those trips, because they’re filled with meaning (family and bestie!).

But when I think of travel, I think of itinerary planning, budgeting, organizing a bunch of cool activities to splurge on, and visiting places I normally wouldn’t get to experience otherwise. The kind of places you only visit a few times, the kind of experiences that remind you just how big the world is and leave you in awe.

Maybe I should’ve picked up more jobs while I was a university student to fund trips like this. But anyway, before I land a full-time role where I’ll need to ask for PTO and coordinate schedules with other full-time friends, I decided that if I had even one friend willing to go with me, I should make it happen.

So I did.

---

## The Flight
<BlogImage src={flight} alt="Flight details for NYC trip" />

Black Friday 2025 came around, and I had already been keeping an eye on flights for January, when prices usually drop after the holidays. I snagged a roundtrip ticket for $287 via Expedia, flying from SJC to LGA. I also booked my hotel through the same platform.

---

## The Stay

Staying in Manhattan, where all the touristy things are, was either too expensive or too basic. My tip for booking hotels is to find one that offers free breakfast, and not just any free breakfast. Read the reviews, or if it’s still unclear, contact the property and ask about portion sizes. Complimentary breakfast can sometimes mean just a muffin and a drink, and that might not quite cut it when you’re hungry.

Choosing **Comfort Inn Prospect Park Brooklyn** was one of the best decisions my travel bestie and I made. For five nights, it came out to $572 total, and splitting it brought it down to $286 each. Breakfast was buffet-style from 7AM to 10AM, and you could take an extra plate to save for later if you wanted to.

Did I mention there was a microwave in the lobby and a refrigerator in the room? And the subway station was just a 7 minute walk away, which made the whole stay feel cozy and convenient. I will say though, we were on the fourth floor, so I’m not sure if the experience differs higher up.

Now that flights and stay are covered, it’s time to plan which attractions are actually worth paying for.

---

## Paid Attractions

### Top of the Rock

<BlogImage src={topofrock} alt="Top of the Rock NYC skyline view" />

A common recommendation is to choose at least one museum and one observatory, and I fully agree. I spent time looking through articles, YouTube videos, and Instagram reels before deciding.

I chose **Top of the Rock** as the observatory. My ticket during Rockefeller Tree season was for one of the cheaper time slots, early morning at 9:50AM, and cost $47. You get unlimited time, but realistically you’ll spend about an hour or two max.

Top of the Rock offers 360 degree views of New York City, and it clearly shows whether you’re looking Uptown or Downtown. My friend and I spent over an hour there, just soaking it all in. I even picked up a cute souvenir to mark the experience.

---

### Broadway Aladdin
<BlogImage src={aladdin} alt="Broadway's Aladdin" />

Broadway shows are a must. But be careful, avoid buying from broadway.com if you can. They’re legit, but significantly more expensive because of added fees. Some tickets end up with 20 plus dollars in unnecessary charges.

Also, those day-of tickets people talk about in Times Square are not always reliable for popular shows, which is why I booked ahead, but not too ahead.

We watched **Aladdin** and bought tickets directly from the official site. We got orchestra seats, aisle, row K, for $99.20 each. Honestly, such a steal because I bought resale tickets.

---

### The MET

<BlogImage src={met} alt="Inside the Metropolitan Museum of Art" />

The MET was ginormous. With the student discount, it cost $17 and gave access to both the MET and the MET Cloisters. The museum itself is so big that you’ll want to set aside at least three hours if you want to take your time (though four or five would be more immersive). It’s beautiful and absolutely worth it.

---

### 9/11 Museum
<BlogImage src={nineeleven} alt="Inside the 9/11 Museum" />

Another attraction that inspired this trip was the 9/11 Museum. It was $30 with student pricing and incredibly informative. When you first enter, there’s a free 25 minute documentary and some real life artifacts from the day of or remains on display along the way. It’s easy to think that’s the main experience, but it’s not.

The main exhibition is the September 11 exhibit, and you should absolutely dedicate at least two hours to it. Don’t spend all your time in the entrance area and rush through the main exhibit, just don’t. That was a mistake, and sadly we did have to rush see the deep history behind 9/11.

---

## Free Attractions

<BlogImage src={ferry} alt="Staten Island Ferry view of Statue of Liberty" />

As for other attractions like the Statue of Liberty, we took the free Staten Island Ferry for a close up view and found that more than enough for our budget. We visited Central Park, but only part of it that was next to the Home Alone hotel. And we stopped by key Home Alone spots.

---

## Transportation
<BlogImage src={subway} alt="Subway Entrance" />
The rest of the trip was a mix of walking and using the OMNY subway system. Fares are $2.90 per ride, but it caps at $35 after 12 rides. You can use the same payment method, I used Google Pay, to reach that cap.

---

## Food

For food, I saved a lot by taking advantage of the hotel breakfast, sometimes combining meals or saving food for later. Most of my spending went toward lunch. Of course, we did treat ourselves to a Korean BBQ meal in Times Square. It was good, but I’ve definitely had better. We went close to closing time, and NYC is very tip heavy. That said, we did get complimentary ice cream at the end, so I’m grateful for that.

---

## Gifts

Lastly, I bought much of my souvenirs at GrandSlam on Times Square, which was this two-story shop filled with keychains, magnets, and postcards.

---

I’ve attached the itinerary I used for reference. And overall, I fell in love with New York City so much I will definitely go again, and take my time.

I stacked my five days because I wanted to see everything I wanted to see in case I don’t go again, but now, if I do go back, I’d go to an observatory at night, like One World, Edge, or Summit One Vanderbilt. I would also watch another Broadway show, Jimmy Fallon, and visit the other side of Central Park. I’d enjoy more food stops like pizza, bagels, and coffee shops (only stopped at one coffee shop which was so good).
